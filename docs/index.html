Bio

This summer, I interned at Matroid where I developed Computer Vision solutions for Matroid studio. I was advised by Reza Zadeh and Danny Jeck. I implemented OpenPose by CMU to run end-to-end inference on Tensorflow GPU for 2D human body pose estimation. Try it out here: Matroid Public Detectors

I am currently pursuing my PhD in Computer Science at University of Maryland Baltimore County advised by Hamed Pirsiavash. My area of research is Computer Vision. I have spent a significant amount of time working on adversarial attacks and defenses - especially for object detection. I have also worked on data poisoning/backdoor attacks on Convolutional Neural Networks which has been accepted for oral presentation at AAAI 2020.

Prior to this, I was a Software Engineer at Samsung Research Institute Bangalore, India where I was part of the DRAM Group in the Memory Team of Samsung Semiconductor India Research.

I spend my time taking photos (find me on Flickr), blogging, playing football, watching good movies and listening to music. I am an avid follower of sports and a supporter of Manchester United FC.
